Diagnosis Details

🪨 KIDNEY STONES

Acute · Non-Traumatic · Imaging & Labs

1. Imaging

📋
Non-Contrast CT Scan  Gold STANDARD A CT scan of the Kidneys, Ureters and Bladder is highly sensitive.
🔊
Ultrasound Especially for pregnant women or younger patients.

2. Laboratory Investigations

🧪
Urinalysis Checked for presence of Hematuria and screen for infection.
🩸
Serum Creatinine & BUN To evaluate kidney function and identify blockage.
🥣
24-hr Urine Collection To analyze chemical composition of the urine.

Key Indicators

Flank → Groin pain Cramping / colicky Radiating Hematuria Acute onset Non-traumatic
